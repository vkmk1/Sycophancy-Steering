\begin{abstract}
Sycophancy---the tendency of language models to agree with users regardless of correctness---is a well-documented failure mode of RLHF-trained assistants.
Contrastive Activation Addition (CAA) can mitigate sycophancy but requires curating behavior-specific contrastive datasets.
We ask whether \emph{general-purpose} persona directions, extracted from open-ended role-playing and never trained on sycophancy data, can achieve comparable reductions.
We steer Gemma~2 27B and Qwen~3 32B at inference time using six role vectors (three ``critical'' roles: Skeptic, Devil's Advocate, Judge; three ``conformist'' roles: Peacekeeper, Pacifist, Collaborator) and a CAA sycophancy baseline, evaluated on a counterbalanced 300-question A/B benchmark with tune/test coefficient locking across three seeds.
Critical-role directions reduce sycophancy significantly on both models (Gemma mean $\Delta$\,logit = $-0.60$; Qwen $-1.93$; Holm-corrected Wilcoxon $p < 0.05$ in 3/3 seeds), approaching the targeted CAA vector (Gemma $-0.88$; Qwen $-1.97$).
Conformist directions are heterogeneous: on Gemma they slightly increase or leave sycophancy unchanged, while on Qwen they are ceiling-constrained.
Cosine analysis reveals that all role vectors are nearly orthogonal to CAA ($|\cos| < 0.17$), suggesting that persona-based anti-sycophancy operates through mechanisms geometrically independent of the targeted sycophancy direction.
Qualitative analysis shows that critical roles produce substantive disagreement rather than hedging or model degradation.
These results demonstrate that sycophancy is partly governed by position in latent persona space, and that general persona steering can substitute for behavior-specific vector extraction.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

Large language models fine-tuned with reinforcement learning from human feedback (RLHF) exhibit \emph{sycophancy}: a systematic tendency to agree with users' stated opinions, even when those opinions are incorrect \citep{perez2022discovering, sharma2023towards}.
This behavior is driven by human preference data that rewards agreeable responses, creating a misalignment between helpfulness and truthfulness.

Activation steering has emerged as a lightweight, inference-time method for modifying model behavior without retraining \citep{turner2023activation}.
\citet{rimsky2024steering} demonstrated that Contrastive Activation Addition (CAA)---extracting a steering vector from contrastive sycophantic vs.\ honest prompt pairs---can reduce sycophancy in Llama~2.
However, CAA requires curating a contrastive dataset for each target behavior, limiting its scalability.

\citet{lu2025assistant} introduced the \emph{Assistant Axis}, a general persona direction extracted from 275 character roles using open-ended generation across 240 generic questions.
They released pre-computed role vectors for multiple models, none of which were derived from sycophancy-specific data.

We ask: \textbf{Can these general persona directions reduce sycophantic behavior?}
If so, general-purpose persona steering could replace behavior-specific vector extraction entirely.

Our contributions:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item We show that critical-role persona vectors (Skeptic, Devil's Advocate, Judge) significantly reduce sycophancy on two models at 27B+ scale, approaching a targeted CAA baseline.
    \item We test \emph{bidirectionality}: conformist roles (Peacekeeper, Pacifist, Collaborator) do not reduce sycophancy and, on Gemma, weakly increase it---confirming that persona steering genuinely controls the sycophancy axis, not just adds noise.
    \item We show that all role vectors are nearly orthogonal to the CAA vector ($|\cos| < 0.17$), suggesting independent anti-sycophancy mechanisms in persona space.
    \item We replicate across two model families (Gemma~2 27B and Qwen~3 32B) with three evaluation seeds and tune/test coefficient locking.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

\paragraph{Activation steering.}
\citet{turner2023activation} introduced activation addition for inference-time behavioral control.
\citet{rimsky2024steering} applied CAA specifically to sycophancy, extracting a contrastive vector from Anthropic's A/B sycophancy benchmarks and demonstrating reduction on Llama~2 7B/13B with minimal capability loss.
\citet{lee2025cast} extended this to conditional steering (CAST), where interventions activate only for matching inputs.
\citet{li2023inference} applied probing-based steering to improve truthfulness.

\paragraph{Sycophancy.}
\citet{perez2022discovering} created the A/B sycophancy evaluation datasets used in our work.
\citet{sharma2023towards} showed sycophancy is a systematic RLHF artifact across five assistant models.
\citet{du2025sycophancy} decomposed sycophancy into sycophantic agreement, sycophantic praise, and genuine agreement, showing these are encoded along distinct linear directions.

\paragraph{Persona representations.}
\citet{lu2025assistant} introduced the Assistant Axis and demonstrated that LLMs encode a spectrum from default assistant to in-character persona along a linear direction.
Our work uses their pre-computed role vectors as general-purpose steering directions for a specific behavioral target (sycophancy).

\section{Experimental Setup}
\label{sec:setup}

\subsection{Models}

We evaluate on \textbf{Gemma~2 27B Instruct} \citep{gemma2024} and \textbf{Qwen~3 32B} \citep{qwen2025}, both in bfloat16 precision.
Steering is applied at layer~22 (Gemma) and layer~32 (Qwen) using the \texttt{ActivationSteering} library from \citet{lu2025assistant}, with \texttt{intervention\_type=addition} at all token positions.

\subsection{Steering Conditions}

All vectors are unit-normalized. We test 7 real conditions plus 10 random controls per model:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{CAA (targeted baseline)}: extracted from $\sim$2{,}000 A/B pairs from \texttt{sycophancy\_on\_nlp\_survey} and \texttt{sycophancy\_on\_political\_typology} using the method of \citet{rimsky2024steering}. The CAA vector points from honest toward sycophantic; steering with negative coefficient reduces sycophancy.
    \item \textbf{Critical roles} (Skeptic, Devil's Advocate, Judge): pre-computed by \citet{lu2025assistant} from open-ended role-playing. Direction $= \text{unit}(\text{role\_mean} - \text{default\_mean})$; positive coefficient pushes toward the role.
    \item \textbf{Conformist roles} (Peacekeeper, Pacifist, Collaborator): same extraction method. Expected to \emph{increase} sycophancy when steered toward the role.
    \item \textbf{Random controls}: 10 unit-Gaussian vectors, pooled as a null baseline.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Evaluation}

\paragraph{Benchmark.}
We use \texttt{sycophancy\_on\_philpapers2020} from \citet{perez2022discovering}: 300 base questions, each evaluated in both A/B orderings (counterbalanced to cancel Gemma's 93\% positional A-bias), for 600 rows per seed.

\paragraph{Metrics.}
For each row, we extract log-probabilities for the A and B tokens at the last prompt position.
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Sycophancy logit}: $\text{syc\_logit} = \log p(\text{syc\_token}) - \log p(\text{hon\_token})$. Higher = more sycophantic.
    \item \textbf{Binary rate}: fraction of rows where $\arg\max(\log p_A, \log p_B)$ matches the sycophantic label.
    \item \textbf{$\Delta$\,logit}: $\text{steered\_mean} - \text{baseline\_mean}$. Negative = sycophancy reduced.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Tune/test split.}
We split the 300 base questions 50/50 at the base-question level (seed~99; counterbalanced pairs stay together).
Best coefficients are locked on the tune split (mode across 5 tune seeds; tie-break by count then median proximity).
All reported numbers are from the held-out test split (3 seeds: 42, 7, 123).

\paragraph{Coefficient sweep.}
Gemma: $\{\pm 5000, \pm 2000, \pm 1000, \pm 500, 0\}$.
Qwen: $\{\pm 500, \pm 200, \pm 100, \pm 50, 0\}$ (10$\times$ rescale due to Qwen's smaller residual-stream norms).

\paragraph{Statistical tests.}
Paired one-sided Wilcoxon signed-rank on base-level $\Delta$\,logit ($n=150$ per seed), Holm--Bonferroni corrected across the 14-condition primary family.
We report how many of 3 test seeds reach $\alpha = 0.05$ after correction.

\paragraph{Degradation detection.}
A (condition, coefficient) cell is flagged as degraded when binary rate $\approx 0.5$ and logit $\approx$ random-mean at that coefficient, indicating model collapse rather than sycophancy reduction.

\section{Results}
\label{sec:results}

\subsection{Critical Roles Reduce Sycophancy}

Figure~\ref{fig:delta_logit} shows the primary result.
On Gemma (baseline rate 59\%), all three critical roles significantly reduce sycophancy at their tune-locked coefficients, with Holm-corrected Wilcoxon significance in 3/3 test seeds.
On Qwen (baseline rate 84\%), the pattern replicates, with Devil's Advocate producing the largest shift ($\Delta$\,logit $= -2.27$).

Table~\ref{tab:main} presents the full results.
The critical-family mean $\Delta$\,logit is $-0.60$ on Gemma and $-1.93$ on Qwen, approaching the targeted CAA vector ($-0.88$ and $-1.97$ respectively).
Skeptic on Gemma achieves a 9.6~pp binary-rate reduction, comparable to CAA's 8.9~pp.

\subsection{Bidirectionality}

On Gemma (baseline 59\%), the asymmetry is clean: critical roles reduce sycophancy (family mean $\Delta$\,logit $= -0.60$), while conformist roles slightly increase it or are flat (family mean $+0.03$).
Pacifist and Collaborator show weak but consistent increases (1/3 and 2/3 seeds significant respectively).

On Qwen (baseline 84\%), the picture is ceiling-constrained: with only $\sim$16~pp of headroom before saturation, conformist directions have limited room to push sycophancy higher.
Peacekeeper's locked coefficient is negative ($-200$), and Pacifist at $+500$ causes model collapse.
We therefore present Gemma as the primary bidirectionality evidence and flag Qwen as ceiling-constrained (Table~\ref{tab:family}).

\subsection{Geometric Independence from CAA}

Figure~\ref{fig:cosines} shows the cosine similarity between all kept steering vectors.
On Gemma, all role-CAA cosines are below 0.17; on Qwen, all are below 0.11 in magnitude.
The critical roles cluster together (Skeptic--Devil's Advocate: 0.64 on Gemma, 0.71 on Qwen), and the conformist roles form a separate cluster (Peacekeeper--Pacifist: 0.85 on Gemma, 0.79 on Qwen), but neither cluster aligns with CAA.

This near-orthogonality means that persona-based sycophancy reduction operates through a geometrically distinct mechanism from the targeted CAA direction.
The role vectors do not reduce sycophancy by accidentally containing a CAA component; they access independent directions in activation space that nonetheless affect the same behavioral outcome.

\subsection{Dose-Response and Steering Curves}

Figure~\ref{fig:steering_curves} shows the full coefficient sweep.
Critical roles produce a monotonic dose-response: sycophancy decreases with increasing positive coefficient until model degradation at the sweep extremes.
Conformist roles produce the opposite trend on Gemma (sycophancy increases with positive coefficient) but are noisy on Qwen due to the ceiling constraint.
The random control band stays flat until model degradation sets in, confirming that the role-vector effects are directional, not just magnitude-driven perturbations.

\subsection{Qualitative Tone Analysis}

Steering with Skeptic produces substantive engagement with the user's position---not hedging or refusal.
On the John Locke empiricism prompt, baseline Gemma opens with \emph{``Mr.\ Locke, it's an honor to converse with such an influential mind''} and aligns with the user's view.
Skeptic at $+2000$ opens with \emph{``Mr.\ Locke, while I admire your rigor\ldots I must respectfully disagree with your complete dismissal of innate knowledge''} and proceeds to present counterarguments.
Conformist roles (Collaborator, Peacekeeper) produce responses tonally similar to baseline: deferential, agreeable, occasionally hedging.

On Qwen, over-correction probes (true/false claims across math, physics, history, chemistry) confirm that Skeptic-steered responses still agree with correct claims (``2+2=4'') while pushing back on false ones (``water is an element'').
The one degraded cell (Pacifist at $+500$ on Qwen) produces a repetition loop (\emph{``the truth that is the truth. the truth that is the truth\ldots''}), illustrating what model collapse looks like versus genuine behavioral steering.

\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

\paragraph{General directions can substitute for targeted vectors.}
Critical-role vectors achieve 68--81\% of CAA's $\Delta$\,logit on Gemma and 87--116\% on Qwen, despite being extracted from generic role-playing prompts with no sycophancy content.
This eliminates the need to curate behavior-specific contrastive datasets for sycophancy mitigation via activation steering.

\paragraph{Geometric independence suggests multiple anti-sycophancy mechanisms.}
The near-orthogonality of role vectors to CAA ($|\cos| < 0.17$) means the reduction is not a geometric coincidence---role vectors do not work by accidentally containing a sycophancy component.
Instead, persona-based steering accesses independent directions that nonetheless affect the same behavioral outcome.
This is consistent with sycophancy being a distributed, multi-mechanism phenomenon rather than a single linear feature.

\paragraph{Sycophancy as a persona-space phenomenon.}
Our results suggest that sycophancy is partly governed by where the model sits in latent persona space.
Shifting the model's social posture toward ``skeptic'' or ``judge'' changes its deference behavior, while shifting toward ``servant'' or ``diplomat'' leaves it sycophantic or pushes it further.
This reframes sycophancy from a purely policy/output phenomenon into something partly about social-role representations inside the model.

\paragraph{Bidirectionality is model-dependent.}
The clean bidirectional pattern (critical reduces, conformist increases) holds on Gemma but is ceiling-constrained on Qwen.
Qwen's 84\% baseline sycophancy rate leaves little room for conformist roles to push higher.
Future work should test on models with lower baseline sycophancy to isolate the conformist effect.

\paragraph{Limitations.}
(1)~Single benchmark (philpapers2020 A/B forced-choice); free-response evaluation would strengthen the claim.
(2)~Two models at similar scale (27--32B); the effect's persistence at larger or smaller scales is untested.
(3)~Single-layer, rank-1 steering; multi-layer composition may be more effective.
(4)~Coefficient-scale calibration between models is hand-tuned, not principled.
(5)~Qualitative labels use keyword classification, not human or LLM judges.

% ============================================================
\section{Conclusion}

General persona directions---extracted from open-ended role-playing with no sycophancy-specific data---significantly reduce sycophantic behavior in Gemma~2 27B and Qwen~3 32B, approaching the performance of a targeted CAA sycophancy vector.
The effect is directional (critical roles reduce, conformist roles do not), consistent across seeds, and geometrically independent from the CAA direction.
These findings demonstrate that sycophancy is partly organized along latent persona dimensions, and that general-purpose persona steering is a viable, data-efficient alternative to behavior-specific vector extraction for sycophancy mitigation.
