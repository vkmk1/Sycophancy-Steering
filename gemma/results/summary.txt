--- Key findings ---
1. Assistant Axis significantly reduces sycophancy (Δlogit = -0.402, paired McNemar p = 0.0000).
2. Targeted role directions outperform the Assistant Axis: Skeptic achieves logit = +0.325 (vs axis +0.678).  Most effective of all tested is Skeptic.
3. Baseline Assistant Axis projection does NOT significantly predict per-question sycophancy preference (Pearson r = -0.088, p = 0.130; Cohen's d between quartiles = -0.380).  Sycophancy does not appear to be cleanly encoded as position along the single default-assistant axis.
4. Steering along the Assistant Axis does measurably shift the residual stream and reduce the sycophancy logit in a dose-dependent way (Spearman ρ = -1.00, p = 0.0000) — but the effect is smaller than targeted role directions, consistent with sycophancy being better characterised by specific role axes (contrarian/skeptic/judge) than by the single default-assistant axis.
